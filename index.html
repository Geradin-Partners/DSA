The Dawn of DSA Enforcement

Lessons from the First Annual Reports from Digital Services Coordinators

Analysis by Geradin Partners

Key Preliminary Findings

Fragmented Readiness

Member States show a wide disparity in legal and institutional readiness, with some fully operational while others lag behind.

Slow Complaint Uptake

While the user complaint mechanism (Article 53) is the most used instrument, uptake remains low in several countries

The 'Irish Funnel'

Ireland has become the central hub for cross-border complaints due to the location of many VLOPs' headquarters.

Nascent Toolkit

Key enforcement tools like out-of-court dispute settlement and researcher data access are not yet fully operational.

Cross-Border Complaints: The 'Irish Funnel'

A large volume of complaints from across the EU are funneled to Ireland's regulator, Coimisiún na Meán.

Common User Grievances

  • Inadequate Statements of Reasons

    Users report account suspensions or content removal without clear, specific reasoning from platforms.

  • Ineffective Internal Complaint Systems

    Failure of platforms' own appeal mechanisms, with users citing difficulties lodging complaints or receiving timely decisions.

  • Lack of Contact Points

    Difficulties in communicating directly with service providers due to inaccessible or missing points of contact.

European Commission’s Role with VLOPs / VLOSEs and overview of first cases opened by the European Commission

While national DSCs handled user complaints, the European Commission exercised its exclusive competence to supervise Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs), focusing on systemic risks.

Looking Ahead

The first year of the DSA has laid the foundation. The coming years will determine if the framework can deliver on its promise of a safer, fairer, and more transparent digital single market by closing implementation gaps, resourcing regulatory bottlenecks, and activating the full enforcement toolkit.
